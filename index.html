About

I'm a PhD student at the Institute for Logic, Language and Computation in Amsterdam. I also spend some of my time at the Centre for Intelligent Systems and their Applications in Edinburgh. My research revolves around ambiguity and categorization, flexibility and convention, as well as economy and equivocation. Amongst others, I'm interested in semantic structure, coordination, reasoning, their interaction, and emergence.

My project is a part of the ESSENCE network (Evolution of Shared Semantics in Computational Environments) and my supervisors are Robert van Rooij and Ewan Klein.

Activities

  • Ongoing organization: DiP Colloquium, 20th Amsterdam Colloquium;
  • Past organization: Logic Tea series, Logic & Language co-chair ESSLLI 2014 student session.

Prior

I received my master's in linguistics at the University of Düsseldorf, where I was advised by Hana Filip and Daniel Altshuler. I also was a research assistant in the CRC 991 (Project B01: "Verb frames at the syntax-semantics interface").
