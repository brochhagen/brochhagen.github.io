About

I am a Marie Curie Early Stage Researcher (PhD student) at the Institute for Logic, Language and Computation in Amsterdam. My research revolves around ambiguity and categorization, flexibility and convention, as well as economy and equivocation.
My project is a part of the ESSENCE network and my supervisor is Robert van Rooij.

I received my B.A. and M.A. in linguistics at the University of Düsseldorf, where I was advised by Hana Filip and Daniel Altshuler. I also was a research assistant in the CRC 991 (Project B01: "Verb frames at the syntax-semantics interface").

Research interests:
semantics & pragmatics; scalarity/gradability; ontology: cognition; ambiguity; evolutionary linguistics; ...
