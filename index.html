Hi, I'm Thomas Brochhagen.

I'm a PhD student at the Institute for Logic, Language and Computation in Amsterdam.

My research revolves around uncertainty in communication and its evolution.

About

I received a master's in linguistics from the University of Düsseldorf, where I was advised by Hana Filip and Daniel Altshuler. I was also a research assistant in the CRC 991 (Project B01: "Verb frames at the syntax-semantics interface").

Since 2014, I conduct research at the Institute for Logic, Language and Computation in Amsterdam. I also spent time at the Centre for Intelligent Systems and their Applications in Edinburgh and the Sony Computer Science Laboratory in Paris.

My research project in Amsterdam was a part of the ESSENCE network, Evolution of Shared Semantics in Computational Environments. My supervisors are Robert van Rooij and Ewan Klein.
